Intel's Sustainability Journey

From the company’s founding to its modern climate goals, Intel has continued working toward better technology, smarter manufacturing, and stronger environmental responsibility.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel begins its journey as a technology company.

The company was founded with a focus on innovation, which later became connected to more efficient computing and responsible manufacturing.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 4004 microprocessor.

This milestone helped make computers smaller and more powerful, changing the future of technology and electronics.

1990s

Cleaner Manufacturing

Intel improves factory and production practices.

Intel began focusing more on reducing waste, improving efficiency, and making manufacturing processes cleaner.

2008

Renewable Energy

Intel increases its use of renewable electricity.

Renewable energy became an important part of Intel’s sustainability efforts, helping reduce the environmental impact of its operations.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE goals.

RISE stands for Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling. It focuses on using technology to create a better future.

2030

Future Goals

Intel works toward long-term sustainability goals.

Intel aims to continue reducing emissions, conserving water, lowering waste, and supporting more sustainable technology.

Project Reflections

Creating a Webpage

The hardest part of creating this webpage was making the timeline responsive. I had to think about how the cards would look on a large screen compared to a phone screen. Flexbox helped because it allowed me to organize the cards in a clean way, and the media query helped the layout change for smaller screens.

Networking

This project showed me that asking for feedback is important when building a website. A person using the page may notice things that I do not notice right away, like text that is hard to read or cards that feel too crowded. Feedback helps make the final design easier for users.

Company Culture

Intel’s sustainability goals show that company culture is not only about products. It is also about responsibility, innovation, and how a company affects people and the environment. A good company culture should care about both success and positive impact.
